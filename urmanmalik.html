Long before Ferguson burst onto the scene as
an express pacer, it was his battles with his
brother that shaped his love for fast bowling.
Their jousts in the car park, and the urge to
be better than his older brother, is what put
him on track to pick up pace bowling. "A spray gun
," is how Mitch Ferguson describes the
early days of Lockie . "But at least he could
bowl fast."
When Lockie turned up for Auckland Aces
for th first time , he made some of the
international players look clueless. Jacob
Oram was one of them . The dream to play for
New Zealand came with its own challenges .
Tim Southee and Trent Boult up front meant
he always had to be the third pacer in the
side. And he's transitioned into a middle-overs
enforcer because of his ability to force batters
into making errors even with an old ball in
hand.
Ferguson , now 30 , talks about his love for fast
bowling , aggression, workload managment,
and more.
