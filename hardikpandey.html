Why Gujarat Titans and
SunRisers Hyderabad are
greater than sum of their parts.
Prasanna Agoram
Wed, 27 Apr 2022
In my past columns, I've often mentioned
the huge importance of the IPL auction,
suggesting that half of the tournament can
be won on the auction table . I still believe
this is true , but as the 2022 IPL plays itself
out, I also believe we are witnessing some
excellent examples of why the auction is only
50 percent of the equation.
After this year's auction , many cricket pundits
(including me) felt that Gujarat Titans looked
the weakest of all the teams. On paper,
they lacked batting fire power and the right
combinations, and the playoffs looked a
distant possibility even before Jason Roy
pulled out of the tournament citing bubble
fatigue.
The Titans has rolled on
relentlessly since their opening win over
Lucknow Super Giants , recording six wins
in their first seven games to top the table
comfortably ahead of other more fancied
teams. How are they managing this with a
team that is not so strong on paper?
